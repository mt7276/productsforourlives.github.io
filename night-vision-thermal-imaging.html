How to choose night vision and thermal devices

When purchasing night vision or thermal imaging devices, you want to be sure and buy something that will meet your needs. If you are looking for a device that will detect objects better, then go for a thermal device. They are able to show you the heat of something, even if hidden behind a bush. If you want to be able to identify objects better, go with night vision. The image returned to the lens allows you to more easily see exactly what you are viewing. Below are a few things to keep in mind when selecting a night vision or thermal imaging device.

  • Budget - Night vision and thermal devices can range from relatively inexpensive to very high priced. Search within your budget and find one that suites your needs.
  • Viewing Range - If you know that you will be searching long fields or large open areas you will want to find a night vision or thermal device that has a further viewing range.
  • Image/Video - Some night vision and thermal devices will take photos and/or videos. If you want to have the ability to do this so that you can show others what you saw or just to save the memories, look for a device that has these options.
  • Storage - If the device is able to take pictures or videos, see what the storage type is and how much. Check if the storage is bult in or if saving requires a memory card. Make sure to read the dscription and see if it incudes a memory card, many buying options do.
  • Zoom - How much can the night vision or thermal device zoom? If you want to take a closer look of something, like an animal, being able to zoom in is a great way to do so without scaring it away. You also have to keep in mind, that after zooming in, there is a possibility of the image not being as clear.
  • Type - Night vision and thermal monoculars are smaller and easier to carry, plus offer one hand use. Binoculars on the other hand are larger and may be harder to carry. Binoculars do offer a wider field of view, which makes them great for watching more at once.
  • Thermal Device Resolution - When shopping for a thermal device, be sure to look at the resolution. A higher resolution means a better and more accurate showing of heat in the image. Thermal devices with a higher resolution can pinpoint smaller things at a further distance.
  • Thermal Device Sensitivity - The better the sensitivity of a device, the more detailed temperatures it can detect. More sensitive devices have a lower NETD(Noise Equivalent Temperature Difference).
  • Battery Life - Always be sure to check the battery life and how long they will last.
