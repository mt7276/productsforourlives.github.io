2. How does a radar gun measure speed?

Radar guns use the Doppler Effect, sending radio waves that bounce off a moving object. The change in frequency between the emitted and reflected waves allows the gun to calculate speed in real time.

3. Can radar guns measure speed through glass or obstacles?

Most radar guns cannot measure accurately through glass, fences, or barriers. Obstructions can distort the signal, reducing accuracy.

For personal, sports, or recreational use, no license is required. Law enforcement devices may require certification depending on jurisdiction.

5. What‚Äôs the difference between Doppler radar and LIDAR?

Doppler radar guns use radio waves, while LIDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) devices use laser beams. LIDAR is often more accurate but is primarily used in law enforcement.
