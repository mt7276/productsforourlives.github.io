‚ö° What Is the Doppler Effect and How Does It Help Measure Speed?

Radar guns emit radio waves that reflect off moving objects. The frequency of the returning signal changes based on the object's motion ‚Äî a phenomenon known as the Doppler shift. By analyzing this shift, radar devices can instantly calculate the object‚Äôs speed.

üìö A Brief History of Radar Gun Technology

Radar was developed in the 1930s and 1940s for military applications during World War II. By the 1970s, sports and law enforcement adopted radar guns for speed tracking ‚Äî starting with Major League Baseball to monitor pitch velocity.

2. How does a radar gun measure speed?

Radar guns use the Doppler Effect, sending radio waves that bounce off a moving object. The change in frequency between the emitted and reflected waves allows the gun to calculate speed in real time.

3. Do radar guns work through a fence or backstop?

Most radar guns cannot measure accurately through glass, chain-link fences, or netting. Obstructions can distort the signal, so place the gun in front of the backstop or use a side angle at open field level.
