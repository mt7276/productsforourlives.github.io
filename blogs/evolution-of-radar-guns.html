Early Days: Baseball Without Speed Tracking

Before radar guns entered the game, baseball coaches and scouts relied completely on the eye test and box-score performance. A pitcher was labeled ‚Äúhard-throwing‚Äù based on reputation, not an actual miles-per-hour reading. There was no accurate way to quantify pitch velocity.

In the early 20th century, a few creative experiments tried to measure pitch speed. In 1912, one test compared a thrown baseball to a motorcycle traveling at a known speed. In the 1930s, Dr. Harold Edgerton used early high-speed photography to estimate ball velocity. These methods were fascinating, but they were too complex and impractical for everyday baseball use.

When Were Radar Guns Invented?

The first radar-based speed measurement systems were invented in the 1940s for military purposes during World War II. These devices used the Doppler effect to calculate the speed of moving objects such as planes. Shortly after, radar technology moved into civilian life and law enforcement, where radar guns were used to measure vehicle speeds on highways.

When Were Radar Guns First Used in Baseball?

Radar guns started appearing around baseball in the 1950s and 1960s when coaches and engineers realized that the same devices used for cars could measure pitch speed. These early attempts worked, but the equipment was bulky and not built specifically for sports.

The true breakthrough for baseball came in 1975 with the release of the first purpose-built baseball radar gun: the JUGS Gun. Created by JUGS Sports, this handheld device was accurate, portable, and instantly useful for tracking pitch velocity during games and bullpen sessions.

When Was the Radar Gun Invented for Baseball?

While general radar guns existed earlier, the radar gun invented for baseball is widely associated with the JUGS Gun in 1975. It was the first widely adopted radar gun specifically marketed and engineered for measuring baseball pitch speeds, and it quickly became standard equipment for coaches, scouts, and training facilities.

When Did MLB Start Using Radar Guns?

Major League Baseball began consistently using radar guns in the late 1970s. By the 1980s, nearly every MLB team relied on radar guns for scouting and player development. Pitch velocity readings soon appeared on stadium scoreboards and television broadcasts, and fans became familiar with how hard their favorite pitchers were throwing.
