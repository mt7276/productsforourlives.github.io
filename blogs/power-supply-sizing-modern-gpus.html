Power Supply Sizing for Modern GPUs

Wattage headroom, ATX 3.0, and 12VHPWR made simple

A stable PSU keeps your gaming PC safe. Here’s how to size wattage, pick ATX 3.0/12VHPWR cables, and avoid tripping OCP with modern GPUs.

Gaming PC Guide PSU Sizing 4-minute read
Read the guide See top gaming PCs

Wattage headroom

  • Target 30–40% headroom over combined CPU+GPU draw to handle transient spikes.
  • Examples: RTX 4070S + i5 = quality 650–750W Gold. RTX 4080S/7900 XTX + i7/i9 = 850–1000W Gold/Platinum.

ATX 3.0 and 12VHPWR

  • ATX 3.0 PSU: Designed for 2x transient loads; preferred for RTX 40 cards.
  • Cable quality: Use native 12VHPWR/12V-2x6 from the PSU, not stacked adapters. Seat fully until it clicks.
  • Bending: Avoid sharp bends within 35–40mm of the connector to prevent heat/brown-out.

Rails, efficiency, and safety

  • Efficiency: 80+ Gold or better for lower heat and noise.
  • Protections: OCP, OVP, OTP, SCP are musts. Stick with reputable brands.
  • Cable count: Use separate PCIe cables for high-draw GPUs if using 8-pin connectors.

Case airflow and thermals

Bottom-mounted PSU should have clear intake and a dust filter. Keep cables tidy to avoid blocking front intake to the GPU. Pair with two front intakes + one rear exhaust for balanced flow.
