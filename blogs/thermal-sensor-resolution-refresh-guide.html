Thermal Sensor Resolution & Refresh Rate Guide

Clarity, detection range, and smooth tracking explained

Thermal image quality is driven by sensor resolution, NETD sensitivity, and refresh rate. Here’s how to choose the right specs for spotting and tracking at night.

Thermal Imaging Specs 4-minute read
Read the guide See top devices

Resolution and lens

  • Sensor resolution: 256×192 is entry; 384×288 mid; 640×480+ for longer-range clarity.
  • Lens FOV: Narrow lenses see farther but with less field of view; wide lenses see more, less detail.

Refresh rate and smoothness

  • 30 Hz is fine for scanning; 50–60 Hz helps when panning fast or tracking moving targets.
  • Higher refresh draws more battery—carry spares or power banks.

NETD and detection

  • NETD (sensitivity): Lower mK values mean better contrast in fog, drizzle, and small temperature differences.
  • Detection vs ID: Specs often list long detection ranges; actual identification range is shorter. Match expectations to lens + resolution.
