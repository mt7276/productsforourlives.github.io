Turning Backyard Catch into Serious Pitching Practice

Baseball has always been a huge part of my life. As a kid, I spent hours throwing in the backyard, practicing my pitches. Back then, the only way to judge my speed was by how fast the ball *looked*. Now, with the help of **radar gun technology**, my son and I can measure every throw with precision.

My **9-year-old son** shares my love for baseball. He's **obsessed with pitching**, and watching him reminds me of myself at his age. But this time, instead of guessing his speed, we have a tool that provides **instant feedback**—our **sports radar gun**.

First Time Using a Radar Gun

When we first used the radar gun, I stood behind our **makeshift home plate**, aiming it at his throws. His **first recorded pitch? 32 MPH.**

That single number changed everything. Seeing his speed motivated him in a way that simple encouragement never could. He immediately wanted to **throw faster**.

Turning Practice into a Fun Competition

To make practice more engaging, we added **game-like drills**:

  • 🎯 **Accuracy + Speed Challenge** – Throwing fast but also hitting the target.
  • 📊 **Consistency Test** – Reaching the same speed multiple times in a row.
  • 🏆 **Personal Best Game** – Trying to beat his previous speed record.

The radar gun gave him **real-time data**. When his speed improved, he got excited. If his velocity dropped, he immediately adjusted his **stance, grip, or windup**.

Breaking 50 MPH – The Big Moment!

At first, my son’s goal was **40 MPH**. Once he hit that, he set his sights on **45 MPH**. Then came the **big milestone**—hitting **50 MPH**.

For weeks, he **fine-tuned his mechanics**—adjusting his **follow-through**, improving his **release point**, and **working on his leg drive**.

Then, one night, after several warm-up pitches, he wound up and fired a fastball. The **radar gun display flashed 52 MPH**. We both went nuts!

How a Radar Gun Makes a Difference

  • 🏆 **Friendly Competition = More Fun** – Each session felt like a game, pushing him to improve.
  • 📈 **Tracking Progress = Confidence Boost** – He could *see* his improvement, which made him train even harder.
