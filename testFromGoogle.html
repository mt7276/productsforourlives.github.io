üìò Handheld GPS Buyer's Guide: Features to Prioritize

Understanding GNSS: Why Multi-Band Accuracy Matters

Traditional GPS receivers use a single frequency (L1) for positioning. **Multi-band** (or dual-frequency) receivers, found in the GPSMAP 65s/67i, use two frequencies (L1 and L5) simultaneously. This is the single biggest upgrade for accuracy, as it effectively filters out signal errors caused by steep canyon walls, dense tree cover, or atmospheric interference. Always prioritize multi-band if you hike in challenging terrain.

Rechargeable vs. AA Battery Power

The choice is simple: **Rechargeable Lithium-Ion** (like the GPSMAP 67i) offers superior runtime, consistency, and cold-weather performance but requires a power bank to recharge. **AA battery models** (like the GPSMAP 65s) are ideal for multi-month expeditions where resupply stops are frequent, as you can carry extra AA batteries without worrying about charging cycles.

Mapping and Storage Capacity

Ensure your GPS unit includes preloaded **TopoActive maps** for your region. Topo maps show elevation contours, which are essential for route planning and understanding the landscape. An **external microSD card slot** is highly recommended if you plan to load custom maps or BirdsEye satellite imagery.
